Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything UK drivers ask before making the switch to electric.

How long does it take to charge an EV at home?

A standard 7kW home charger will fully charge most EVs overnight (6–10 hours). A rapid public charger (50–350kW) can add 100 miles in as little as 20–30 minutes.

Is there a government grant for home EV chargers?

Yes — the OZEV Electric Vehicle Chargepoint Grant provides up to £350 off the cost of a home charger for eligible properties. Renters and flat owners can also apply.

What is the cheapest electric car in the UK?

The Dacia Spring starts from around £14,995, making it the most affordable new EV in the UK. The Renault 5 and MG4 offer the best combination of price and range.

Do electric cars pay road tax (VED) in the UK?

From April 2025, zero-emission vehicles pay a flat rate of £195 per year (the same as petrol/diesel cars). Before that date, EVs were exempt from VED entirely.

Can I charge an EV with a standard 3-pin plug?

Yes, in an emergency. A standard 3-pin plug charges at around 2.3kW — fine for topping up overnight but slow for daily charging. A dedicated home charger is strongly recommended.

How does EV battery degradation work?

Modern EV batteries typically retain 80–90% of their capacity after 100,000 miles. Most manufacturers offer an 8-year battery warranty. Avoiding frequent rapid-charging helps preserve longevity.
